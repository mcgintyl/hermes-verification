# M33 Baryonic Convention Sensitivity — Provenance Clarification Addendum

**Locked result status:** unchanged. This addendum clarifies labeling only. It does not alter the archived Hermes result, the MOND comparator result, the frozen M33 board, or any diagnostic numbers.

## Clarification

The $f\approx1.21$ branch in the M33 baryonic-convention sensitivity diagnostic is **not** a true SPARC-equivalent unit-M/L board.

Corbelli's public Table 1 gives a stellar **mass surface-density profile** derived from pixel-SED / stellar-population modeling. It does not directly provide the raw 3.6 μm or W1 light profile required to reconstruct a literal SPARC-style $\Upsilon_\star=1$ stellar component from luminosity.

Therefore, the $M/L=1$ diagnostic branch should be labeled:

> **SPARC-like unit-M/L diagnostic analogue / stellar-mass convention sensitivity branch.**

It should not be labeled:

> SPARC-equivalent rotmod reconstruction, literal $\Upsilon_\star=1$ board, or replacement for the locked source-native M33 board.

## Locked board label

The board label remains:

> **M33-Corbelli source-native thick-disk reconstruction**

with source-native SPS/pixel-SED stellar mass, radially varying M/L provenance, helium factor 1.33, H$_2$ prescription, flaring stellar disk, gas half-thickness 0.5 kpc, and $V_{\rm bul}=0$.

## Practical meaning

The $f\approx1.21$ branch estimates what a near-IR unit-M/L shift might do, based on the inferred effective baseline $M/L\approx0.683$. It is a convention-sensitivity diagnostic, not an independent reconstruction of the SPARC rotmod convention.
