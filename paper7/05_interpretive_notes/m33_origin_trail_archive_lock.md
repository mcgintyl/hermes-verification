# M33 Outer-Disk Origin-Trail Addendum — Archive Lock

**Archive classification:** M33 Outer-Disk Origin-Trail Addendum — moderate support for a multi-origin gravitational-biography boundary case.

**Locked status:** accepted as the final interpretive provenance note for M33 unless new data arrives.

## Accepted interpretation

The supported claim is not that the entire M33 outer disk is proven non-native. The supported claim is narrower:

> M33’s outer disk is an externally disturbed, accretion-sensitive, multi-component regime whose gas and stars do not cleanly share the same history as the inner disk.

## Accepted points

1. The Hermes stress zone overlaps the radius where M33’s outer disk changes character.
2. The H I warp and perturbed outer rotation begin around ~8 kpc and extend into the same radial range as the board’s outer-tail stress.
3. The stellar disk break and far-outer CMD fields at ~9–12 kpc overlap the transition into the Hermes failure zone.
4. The gas-origin trail includes disturbed H I, arcs/filaments, clouds, possible return-flow, M31 interaction context, and recent gas-accretion / cosmic-filament scenarios.
5. The stellar-origin trail is mixed: inner disk inside-out growth, outer age-gradient reversal, old metal-poor far-outer populations, and possible accreted/migrated stellar material.
6. Therefore the one-galaxy/one-age assumption is astrophysically fragile for M33’s outer disk.

## Paper-safe framing

The M33 origin-trail audit strengthens the interpretation that the locked Hermes residual is spatially and astrophysically localized. Hermes fits the main disk cleanly, but the stressed R > 10 kpc region overlaps M33’s warp onset, disk break, far-outer CMD population reversal, disturbed H I, off-rotation gas, and published accretion or interaction scenarios. This does not erase the fixed-MOND comparator result, which remains clean on the same frozen board, and it does not prove that Hermes is correct to reject the outer disk. Rather, it identifies M33 as a boundary case for the current one-galaxy/one-age implementation. A future dynamic-aging Hermes extension would need to treat component origin, radial age, and circular-equilibrium status explicitly; that extension is outside the executable scope of the present paper.

## Final archive sentence

M33 is not merely a miss; it is a localized, physically interpretable stress case: a source-native external board where Hermes ports cleanly across the main disk but fails in an outer regime independently associated with warp, disk-break, disturbed H I, and accretion-sensitive/multi-origin structure, while MOND remains clean on the same frozen board.
