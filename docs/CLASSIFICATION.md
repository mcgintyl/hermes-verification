The paper classifies 133 galaxies into four structural categories using two variables — peak observed rotation speed (vmax) and median bulge fraction (f_bul):

Bulge-dominated (n=26): Median bulge fraction > 0.05
Dwarf (n=31): vmax < 80 km/s, no significant bulge
Giant (n=15): vmax > 200 km/s, no significant bulge
Spiral (n=61): Intermediate rotation speed (80–200 km/s), no significant bulge

Bulge fraction is evaluated first. Among galaxies without significant bulges, the classification is determined by peak rotation speed. The full per-galaxy assignments are in Hermes_ConfigG_PerGalaxy_133_Export.csv.
The young galaxy calibration subset (Table S3) consists of 22 galaxies with ψ < 0.20 (β > 2.18).
